"Cat" can refer to a domesticated feline animal (Felis catus) or to the Common Admission Test (CAT) exam for postgraduate management programs in India. The animal cat is a small, carnivorous mammal known for its independence and ability to purr. The CAT exam is a highly competitive entrance test for MBA programs at top business schools in India. Cat as a Domesticated Feline: Animal: Cats are small, furry mammals belonging to the family Felidae. Domesticated: They have been domesticated for nearly 10,000 years. Carnivorous: They are meat-eaters. Traits: Known for their independence, purring, and playful nature. Cat as the Common Admission Test (CAT): Exam: CAT is a computer-based exam conducted by IIM (Indian Institutes of Management) to shortlist candidates for postgraduate programs. Purpose: It is used to evaluate candidates for MBA and other management programs at various business schools in India. Sections: The CAT exam consists of three sections: VARC (Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension), DILR (Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning), and Quantitative Ability.
