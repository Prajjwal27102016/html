A lion, often referred to as the "King of the Jungle," is a large, powerful cat native to Africa, known for its majestic mane on adult males and social living within groups called prides; these prides consist of related females, cubs, and a few dominant males who primarily defend the territory, while the females are the primary hunters, preying on large herbivores like zebras and wildebeest; due to their apex predator status, lions play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystem, but unfortunately face conservation challenges due to habitat loss and poaching. Key points about lions: Physical features: Muscular build, large head, short legs, distinctive mane on males, tawny colored fur with a dark tuft at the tail tip. Social structure: Live in prides, with related females staying together for life, while males may roam to find new prides to take over. Hunting behavior: Primarily female hunters, using coordinated strategies to take down large prey. Conservation status: Considered vulnerable due to declining populations.
