The tiger is India's national animal because of its strength, agility, and grace. It is also a symbol of India's pride and natural resources. Appearance: The tiger has a thick yellow coat with dark stripes It is the largest of all cats It has a long tail with black stripes Habitat: Tigers live in tropical forests, grasslands, mountains, and swamplands They are found in India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh Conservation: The tiger population in India was declining, so the government launched Project Tiger in 1973 The project has established tiger reserves and increased the tiger population in India Hunting and poaching: Tigers were exported to other countries during British rule in India Foreign invaders and rulers considered hunting tigers to be a symbol of masculinity Tigers are threatened by poaching and habitat loss Role in the ecosystem: Tigers are apex predators that help regulate the population of other animals This helps to maintain the stability of the ecosystem Other facts: Tigers are known for their ability to leap, swim, and spring They learn to hunt from their mothers They are ambush predators that wait for prey at watering holes
